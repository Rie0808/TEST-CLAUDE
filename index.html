She spent twenty minutes asking questions and reverse image-searching on Google before she could even open Grab — then she had to run interference between the driver and FIL the whole way there.

Julie

Thirty minutes, twice a day

Julie's mum has early-stage dementia. Each day, Aunty G's part-time helper walks her down and pings Julie to call a Grab. Julie books it, briefs the driver about the dementia, monitors the route, and pings her own helper to be downstairs in time to receive her mum. Then again in reverse, every evening.

Pain: For thirty minutes, twice a day, Julie's life stops. She's the human relay between four people who can't message each other directly.

HuiN

Meeting them at the door

HuiN's dad has many medical appointments. Her mum can't coordinate them, so HuiN holds the calendar. What we heard

The caregiver has become the dispatcher.

Three different families. One shared truth: the people doing the caregiving are also doing the logistics layer around it — and that layer is invisible work.

Destination research. Driver briefing. Live route monitoring. Pickup-side handoffs. Drop-off-side receivers. Rendezvous timing. Re-booking when things slip. None of this is the caregiving — but all of it falls on the caregiver, because no one else has the context to do it.

It happens during their work hours. It fragments their attention. It can't be delegated, because the coordination *is* the work.

"For thirty minutes, my life stops." — Julie

The intervention

Meet Carebear

Carebear is a personal assistant for caregivers. It holds the schedule of care for everyone you look after, and quietly handles the logistics around it. The medical appointments, the daily transfers, the helpers, the rides — one place, one tap, one less thing to hold in your head.

01

Add an appointment

"Dad — cardiologist, Thursday 3pm." Carebear asks the questions once: which clinic, return trip, prep needed, who's meeting him there. Saved forever.

02

Carebear builds the timeline

Reminders, helper pings, ride bookings, fasting alerts — all scheduled automatically from the appointment. You don't manage the timeline; the timeline manages itself.

03

One tap to Grab

When it's time to book, Carebear opens Grab with pickup, drop-off, and driver briefing already filled in. You confirm. Done.

04

The right pings, only when they matter

Pickup-helper gets notified to bring them down. Receiver gets notified at drop-off. You get notified only if something goes off-script.

How Carebear works

The pieces

Care Circle

One caregiver, many people to look after. Profiles for each — conditions, languages, helpers, emergency contacts. Set up once.

Smart Places

"FIL's TCM in Hougang" is saved the first time, used forever. Aliases, accessibility notes, the doctor's name. Kills the Google-image-search loop.

Events & roles

Medical appointments, recurring transfers, one-off outings — all the same shape. Each event knows who's the passenger, who hands off, who receives, who meets them there.

Rendezvous sync

When you're meeting them at the destination, Carebear sequences both rides so you arrive together. Two Grabs, one synced arrival.

Auto driver briefing

"Passenger has early-stage dementia, please confirm pickup with me at +65..." Pre-written, one-tap-sent. No retyping per trip.

Exception-only alerts

You get pinged when something deviates — delay, off-route, long stop. Otherwise Carebear just runs in the background. Your attention is precious.

For Grab

A funnel for recurring, high-value trips

Singapore is aging fast. Every caregiver Carebear supports generates predictable, repeatable ride demand — trips that today are either friction-laden bookings or leaking entirely to taxis and family driving.

Volume

A typical caregiver on Carebear generates between 2 and 20+ trips per week per care recipient — medical visits, daily transfers, return legs, and the caregiver's own rendezvous trips.

Premium tier

Carebear creates a natural home for Grab Care — opt-in drivers trained for elderly and special-needs passengers. New margin, new differentiation.

Stickiness

Once a caregiver has set up profiles, places, and recurring events in Carebear, switching ride apps means rebuilding everything. Highest-LTV users in the network.

Carebear captures the schedule. Grab captures the trips it generates.
